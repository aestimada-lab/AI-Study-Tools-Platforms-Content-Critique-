The generated statements were manually verified using government reports, international organization publications, and peer-reviewed literature.

### 2. Critical Reflection on Tool Limitations

The AI platform successfully condensed large volumes of literature related to women's safety, gender-responsive governance, and digital intervention systems into concise summaries. This significantly reduced the time required to identify major themes and potential research directions.

However, the audit process revealed several limitations. Some statements exaggerated the availability of digital safety interventions in rural communities, while others generalized findings from broader international studies without considering local conditions in Mindanao. Certain claims also presented projected outcomes as established facts, creating the risk of misinformation.

The verification process demonstrated that AI-generated literature reviews should be treated as preliminary research aids rather than authoritative sources. Human review remains essential for validating statistics, confirming citations, evaluating contextual relevance, and distinguishing evidence-based findings from unsupported conclusions. Through manual verification, the overall reliability and academic integrity of the literature synthesis were substantially improved.

---

### 3. Key Research Insights

- Violence against women remains a significant development concern in the Philippines and continues to require coordinated intervention efforts.
- Digital technologies can improve access to information, reporting systems, and support services for women.
- Mobile applications are most effective when integrated with existing government, community, and emergency response systems.
- Women's empowerment initiatives benefit from combining safety mechanisms with economic and educational opportunities.
- Human verification is necessary to ensure the accuracy, credibility, and policy relevance of AI-generated research outputs.

### Conclusion

The audit confirmed that AI tools can efficiently synthesize large volumes of literature but may introduce factual inaccuracies, unsupported assumptions, and contextual errors. By systematically verifying AI-generated claims against authoritative sources, researchers can maintain academic rigor while benefiting from the speed and efficiency of artificial intelligence. The findings support the potential value of mobile-based women's safety and empowerment systems in Samal Island, provided that such interventions are implemented alongside broader institutional and community-based support mechanisms.
